I see Bill sitting, I go up to him to say good-bye. "I will write you." Carol, his wife, dances wildly with a blind girl who swirls around till she is out of breath. She tries to hide her head inside a young man's shirt. We hold her hand and comfort her. "Such great love and giving," I say. -- A woman with kids swims with a man with kids: both are widowed. I receive a key to a very modern store: books, records, from the Jung Society, but Joyce V. takes it to go in and fry ham. I see Zena in a huge crowd of people. I follow her and lose her, then find her playing with Bill &amp; Carol's kids.
